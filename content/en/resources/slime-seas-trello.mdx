## A-Tier: Specialized Powerhouses

A-tier races are excellent choices if you have a specific weapon or fighting style in mind. While they might lack the universal "god-mode" feel of the S-tier, they excel in their respective niches.

### Oni and Hollow
The Oni race is the gold standard for pure melee builds that don't have access to the Servant race. Its 10% damage reduction and 20% melee damage multiplier make it incredibly beefy. Conversely, the Hollow race is the go-to for players utilizing the Ichigo sword. It provides a specific 20% boost to that weapon, which, when combined with its 60% HP buff, creates a high-impact offensive build.

## B-Tier: Reliable Mid-Game Options

If you find yourself with a B-tier race, you don't necessarily need to reroll immediately, especially if you are still leveling. These races provide decent stats but will eventually be outclassed by the legendary variants listed on the **slime seas trello**.

*   **Vampire:** Great for sustain with 5% life steal, but the 30% HP buff is relatively low for late-game bossing.
*   **Fishman:** A unique utility pick. It offers no combat stats but increases money and experience gain by 15%. It is the best race for "power leveling" before switching to a combat-focused race.
*   **Shinagami:** Specifically tuned for Eisen sword users. It offers a solid 50% HP boost and 45% overall damage.
*   **Shadoworn:** The "budget" version of the Hollow race, designed for Shadow Sword users with a 20% damage boost to that specific weapon.

## C and D Tier: The Reroll Zone

Races in these tiers are generally considered "starter" races. While they are technically better than being a Human, they offer very little in terms of scaling. According to the **slime seas trello** data, the stat differences here make high-level content significantly more difficult.

1.  **Mink:** Focuses entirely on mobility (+20% speed and jump). While fun for exploration, the lack of damage and HP makes it a liability in raids.
2.  **Orc:** A basic 15% HP and 15% damage buff. It is functional but boring.
3.  **Demon:** Slightly better than Orc due to a 20% sprint speed boost, but still lacks the impact of higher tiers.
4.  **Skypa:** Provides two extra jumps. This is almost entirely useless in the 2026 combat meta where positioning is handled by skills rather than basic jumping.

> **Warning:** Never settle for the Human race. It provides 0% stat bonuses and is essentially a "blank" slot. Always use your rerolls to move away from Human as soon as possible.

## Strategy: When to Reroll Your Race

Rerolling is a core mechanic, but doing it efficiently is key to progressing without wasting resources. Based on the 2026 meta, here is the optimal strategy:

**Early Game (Levels 1-500):**
If you roll **Fishman**, keep it. The 15% XP boost will shave hours off your grind to the level cap. If you roll anything else in the B-tier or higher, stay with it until you reach the second sea.

**Mid Game (Levels 500-1500):**
This is where you want to aim for **Kitsune** or **Oni**. You need the extra HP to survive mid-tier bosses. Kitsune is especially valuable here to help you drop the gear needed for the end-game.

**End Game (Level 1500+):**
At this stage, you should only be satisfied with **Slime, Servant, or Leviathan**. The 10% damage reduction and high life steal percentages found in these races are mandatory for the 2026 raid content. Check the **slime seas trello** frequently during this stage to see if any new "Ancient" races have been discovered.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the official slime seas trello link?**

A: The link is typically hosted in the official game description or the community Discord server. It is updated frequently by the developers to reflect the 2026 balance changes.

**Q: Is the Slime race better than the Servant race?**

A: It depends on your weapon. Slime is superior for sword builds due to the 17% sword multiplier. Servant is better for melee/fighting style builds because of its 17% melee multiplier and slightly higher HP pool.

**Q: Does the Kitsune luck multiplier affect boss drops?**

A: Yes, the 25% luck multiplier applies to almost all RNG-based drops, including rare swords, accessories, and even fruit spawns.

**Q: How do I get more rerolls for my race?**

A: Rerolls can be obtained through codes, purchased with in-game currency at specific NPCs, or bought with Robux. Always check the Trello for active codes that might provide free rerolls.
