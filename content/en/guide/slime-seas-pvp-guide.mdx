## Understanding the PvP Stat Caps

One of the most important things to realize before entering a fight is that Slime Seas implements a "Power Floor and Ceiling" system. This means that no matter how much you have grinded your base stats in the open world, the game enforces strict limits the moment you engage in a PvP encounter. This normalization ensures that fights aren't decided purely by who spent more hours farming slimes, but rather by who utilizes their skills more effectively.

Currently, every player is locked into a specific set of baseline attributes. You cannot exceed these limits regardless of your equipment buffs or temporary potions.

| Attribute | PvP Cap Value | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Health (HP)** | 10,000 | The universal baseline for all classes. |
| **Melee Damage** | 325 | The standard hit value for basic M1 attacks. |
| **Level Requirement** | 85 | The recommended level to max out potential. |
| **Max Mastery** | Level 5 | Required to unlock the highest damage multipliers. |

Because health is capped at 10,000, calculating your "Burst Potential" becomes a mathematical necessity. If your combo can deal 5,000 damage, you know you only need two successful rotations to secure a kill.

## The Katana: King of Burst Damage

For players who prefer a "glass cannon" or high-mobility playstyle, the Katana remains the undisputed king of the 2026 meta. The Katana relies on rapid-fire strikes and closing the gap instantly. At Mastery 5, the Katana transforms from a standard blade into a weapon capable of deleting half a health bar in a single sequence.

The core of the Katana’s power lies in the "Thousand Slashes" ability. When you reach Mastery 5, this move deals a staggering 2,700 damage. When paired with secondary skills, the burst is nearly impossible to out-heal.

### Katana Skill Synergy

| Skill Name | Mastery Level | Damage Output | Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Thousand Slashes** | 5 | **2,700** | Primary burst/finisher move. |
| **Surge Wave** | 3 | **1,600** | Mid-range projectile for pressure. |
| **Flashblade Surge** | 0-3 | **800** | Gap closer and combo extender. |

> **Warning:** The Katana is highly reliant on landing your Thousand Slashes. If you miss this high-cooldown move, you lose nearly 30% of your total rotation damage, leaving you vulnerable to counter-attacks.

To maximize this build, always lead with *Flashblade Surge* to stun the opponent, follow up with *Surge Wave* to chip their health, and then unleash *Thousand Slashes* once they have exhausted their dash or dodge frames.

## The Scythe: High Skill, High Reward

The Scythe is often considered the most difficult weapon to master in Slime Seas. Unlike the Katana, which focuses on raw burst, the Scythe is about area denial and "Death's Grasp" mechanics. It requires precise timing and a deep understanding of your opponent's movement patterns.

The Scythe excels at Mastery 5, specifically with the *Void Surge* ability. While its individual damage numbers might look lower than the Katana's on paper, the Scythe's ability to pull enemies in and disrupt their rhythm makes it a nightmare in the hands of a skilled player.

### Scythe Combat Breakdown

*   **Death's Grasp (Mastery 0):** This is your essential opener. Dealing 700 damage, it serves as a hook to pull targets toward you, setting up your more powerful strikes.
*   **Spinning Strike (Mastery 3):** A reliable 1,300 damage move that provides 360-degree coverage, making it great for fending off aggressive Berserker players.
*   **Void Surge (Mastery 5):** Your ultimate tool, dealing 1,200 damage while applying a brief displacement effect.

If you enjoy a tactical approach where you control the pace of the fight, the Scythe is your best bet. Just ensure you are practicing your "hook" shots with Death's Grasp, as missing your opener often means losing the trade.

## Greatsword: The Pressure Machine

If you prefer a playstyle that focuses on "Consistent Pressure," the Greatsword is the tool for the job. It is currently one of the most reliable weapons in the game because its hitboxes are massive and its damage is difficult to avoid. While it lacks the extreme burst of the Katana, it makes up for it with sheer "broken" pressure that forces opponents to play defensively.

| Skill Name | Mastery Level | Damage | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Spiraling** | 5 | **1,700** | Multi-hit move that punishes blocks. |
| **Sweeping Slam** | 3 | **1,300** | Massive AOE that is hard to dodge. |
| **Earthbreaker** | 0 | **900** | Perfect for starting a combo. |

The Greatsword's greatest strength is *Earthbreaker*. Because it deals 900 damage even at Mastery 0, it is one of the strongest "base" moves in the game. You can use it right out of the gate to force an opponent to use their defensive cooldowns early.

## The Berserker Class: Pure Aggression

The Berserker is the go-to class for players who want to stay in their opponent's face. It features some of the highest "floor" damage in the game, meaning you don't need high mastery levels to start being a threat. This makes it an excellent choice for players currently grinding toward level 85.

The standout move for the Berserker is *Impaling Thrust*. Even at Mastery 0, it deals 1,200 damage, matching the Mastery 5 moves of other high-tier weapons.

### Berserker Skill Priority
1.  **Impaling Thrust (1,200 Damage):** Use this to pierce through enemy defenses and start your chain.
2.  **Colossal Slam (1,200 Damage):** Requires Mastery 5. This is your heavy hitter that deals massive impact damage.
3.  **Leaping Slam (900 Damage):** Use this for vertical mobility or to catch players who are trying to jump away from your pressure.

> **💡 Tip:** As a Berserker, your goal is to never let the opponent breathe. Use your slams to stay mobile and keep the pressure on until their 10,000 HP bar is depleted.

## FAQ

**Q: Why is my damage lower in PvP than in the open world?**

A: Slime Seas uses a stat-capping system for PvP. Your health is limited to 10,000 and your melee damage is capped at 325 to ensure fair competition. Your true power comes from your weapon mastery levels rather than your base stats.

**Q: What is the best weapon for a beginner in the Slime Seas PvP guide?**

A: The Greatsword or the Berserker class are excellent for beginners. Both offer high damage moves (like Earthbreaker and Impaling Thrust) at Mastery 0, allowing you to be competitive even while you are still grinding for Mastery 5.

**Q: How do I increase my weapon mastery quickly?**

A: Mastery is increased by using the weapon in combat. The fastest way is to participate in high-level mob farming or frequent PvP duels. Reaching level 85 first is recommended so you can focus entirely on your mastery levels.

**Q: Is the Katana better than the Scythe?**

A: It depends on your playstyle. The Katana offers higher burst damage (2,700 with Thousand Slashes), making it better for quick kills. The Scythe offers better control and displacement, making it superior for players who like to dictate the movement of the fight.
