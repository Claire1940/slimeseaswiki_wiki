## Slime Piece Locations 1 through 3: The Early Islands

The first leg of your journey through the **slime seas islands** takes you to some of the most recognizable landmarks in the game. These are generally easier to access but require you to look behind objects and inside structures.

### 1. The Desert Island (Skeleton Ribs)
Your first stop should be the Desert Island. This arid landscape is home to massive skeletal remains. To find the first slime piece, head toward the large rib cage structure protruding from the sand. The slime is nestled right against one of the "rib" bones. It is bright and distinct against the yellow sand, making it a great starting point for your collection.

### 2. The Snow Island (Hidden Tree)
Next, use your teleportation map to head to the Snow Island. This area is characterized by constant snowfall and dense pine trees. From the spawn point, run toward the back of the island. You are looking for a specific tree that stands slightly apart from the rest. The second slime piece is tucked directly behind the trunk, obscured from the main path.

### 3. The Starter Island (The House)
Surprisingly, one of the pieces is hidden right where many players begin their journey. Return to the Starter Island and look for the residential buildings. You will need to enter the specific house located near the center of the village. Inside, you will find the third slime piece sitting on the floor, waiting to be claimed.

## Slime Piece Locations 4 and 5: Verticality and Urban Centers

As you progress through the **slime seas islands**, the hiding spots become a bit more vertical. You will need to utilize your jumping abilities or flight skills to reach these next two pieces.

### 4. Jungle Island (Sniper Tower)
The Jungle Island is a lush, green environment filled with vertical platforms. To find the fourth piece, locate the tall Sniper Tower. This structure overlooks the jungle canopy. You will need to climb or fly to the very top platform of the tower. The slime piece is located right on the roof, offering a great view of the surrounding **slime seas islands**.

### 5. The City/Station Area (Behind Gojo)
The fifth piece is located in the bustling City area, often referred to as the Station. This is a more urban environment with multiple buildings. From the main spawn point, head toward the area where the Gojo boss resides. Do not engage the boss; instead, move behind the large building adjacent to the boss arena. The slime piece is tucked into a corner behind the architecture, making it easy to miss if you are just passing through.

## Slime Piece Locations 6 and 7: The Final Stretch

The final two pieces are located in areas that require a bit of exploration off the beaten path. These locations represent the "outer" reaches of the **slime seas islands** questline.

### 6. The Pipe Island (Sewage Pipes)
Teleport to the island known for its industrial aesthetic and large sewage pipes. This location can be a bit confusing due to the similar-looking structures. Look for the set of parallel pipes running along the ground. You will find the sixth slime piece wedged directly between two of these pipes. If you get stuck in the geometry (a common occurrence here), use your "Reset Character" or "Teleport" function to free yourself.

### 7. The Ruins (The Wooden Tree)
The final piece is located at the Ruins. This island is filled with crumbling stone structures and ancient vibes. Head to the back of the largest ruined building. You are looking for a specific wooden tree that grows near the stone walls. The seventh slime piece is located at the base of this tree, hidden by the overgrown foliage.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to find the slime pieces in a specific order?**

A: No, you can collect the pieces across the **slime seas islands** in any order that suits your travel path. However, following the order in this guide is generally the fastest route based on island proximity.

**Q: What happens if a slime piece doesn't appear for me?**

A: First, ensure you have the active quest in your log. If the quest is active and the piece is missing, try hopping to a different server. Sometimes, localized lag can prevent quest items from rendering correctly.

**Q: Can I trade slime pieces with other players?**

A: No, slime pieces are quest-specific items and are bound to your character. You must physically visit the **slime seas islands** locations yourself to collect them.

**Q: Is there a level requirement to start the Slime Quest?**

A: While there is no strict level requirement to pick up the pieces, it is recommended to be at least level 50 so you have access to the necessary teleportation waypoints and can survive the mobs on the later islands.
