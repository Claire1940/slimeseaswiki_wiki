## S-Tier: The Best Races to Keep

If you land on one of these races during your **slime seas race reroll** session, you should stop immediately. These races offer the most comprehensive stat packages in the game.

### Dragonborn
Dragonborn remains the undisputed king of Slime Seas in 2026. It is the most versatile race because it boosts three different weapon types while providing a massive boost to survivability. The 15% skill cooldown speed is particularly valuable for players who rely on high-damage abilities to clear dungeons.

*   **15% Skill Cooldown Speed**
*   **25% Max Health**
*   **20% Scythe Damage**
*   **10% Fire Damage**
*   **20% Greatsword Damage**

### Lunarian
For players who prefer the fast-paced combat of katanas, Lunarian is the top choice. It combines movement speed with solid defensive stats, making it feel incredibly "smooth" in active combat. The fire damage bonus also synergizes well with many elemental katanas.

*   **10% Movement Speed**
*   **10% Max Health**
*   **20% Katana Damage**
*   **10% Fire Damage**

## A-Tier: Strong Specialized Options

A-tier races are excellent and often worth keeping, especially if they align with your preferred weapon. However, they may lack the sheer defensive power of the S-tier options.

### Jin
Jin is the go-to race for "hit and run" playstyles. With a 25% increase to dash distance, you can weave in and out of boss attacks with ease. The primary drawback is the lack of any health or defensive buffs, making it a "glass cannon" choice for scythe users.

### Elf
Elf is the definition of a balanced race. It doesn't have the extreme highs of Dragonborn, but it has no real weaknesses. It is a very safe race to settle on if you are running a katana build and want a mix of speed and shorter cooldowns.

### Shadowborn
Shadowborn is the most polarizing race in the game. It offers the highest katana damage bonus (35%) and the best mobility, but the penalties are severe. Losing 25% of your max health makes you incredibly fragile in 2026's endgame content.

> **💡 Tip:** Only keep Shadowborn if you are highly skilled at dodging. The -50% damage penalty to Greatswords and Scythes means you are permanently locked into using Katanas.

## B-Tier and C-Tier: Situational Picks

These races are generally considered "stepping stones." While they are better than being Human, you should eventually aim for a **slime seas race reroll** to find something more powerful.

*   **Oni:** A solid choice for Greatsword users who want to be tanky. However, the -5% movement speed penalty can make dodging boss mechanics frustratingly difficult.
*   **Skypian:** A basic race that provides 10% katana damage. It is fine for the early game but is completely outclassed by Lunarian and Elf.
*   **Fishman:** Unless the developers add more underwater-focused content in late 2026, the 50% swim speed is almost useless in standard combat.

## D-Tier: The Reroll Fodder

### Human
Human is the default race and provides a 5% XP gain. While this sounds helpful for leveling, the combat benefits of every other race far outweigh a small experience boost. If you are playing [Slime Seas on Roblox](https://www.roblox.com/), your first priority should be moving away from the Human race as soon as possible.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more race rerolls in Slime Seas?**

A: Rerolls can typically be obtained through in-game codes, purchasing them with Robux, or completing specific high-level quests and achievements. Always check the official social media channels for the latest active codes.

**Q: Is Shadowborn better than Dragonborn for Katana users?**

A: Purely for damage, Shadowborn is stronger due to the 35% Katana boost. However, Dragonborn is generally considered better for most players because the 25% health and 15% cooldown reduction provide much better survivability in late-game raids.

**Q: Does the slime seas race reroll affect my level or progress?**

A: No, rerolling your race only changes your racial passives and appearance. Your character level, weapon mastery, and inventory will remain exactly the same.

**Q: What is the rarest race to get from a reroll?**

A: While exact percentages vary by update, "Legendary" tier races like Dragonborn and Shadowborn have significantly lower drop rates compared to Common or Rare races like Human or Skypian. Be prepared to use several dozen rerolls if you are hunting for a specific S-tier race.
