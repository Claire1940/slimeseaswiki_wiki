## Locating the Crimson Ruins Island

The journey to find your first major pet begins at the **Crimson Ruins Island**. This location is strategically placed to be accessible early in your progression, but it poses a significant threat to those who are unprepared. While the island itself has a recommended entry level, the actual boss fight you must engage in is considerably more difficult.

| Island Feature | Details |

## Summoning and Defeating the Crimson Demon

The **slime seas baby azure drake** cannot be purchased from a standard vendor; it must be earned through combat. The source of the Drake Egg is the Crimson Demon, a mini-boss that requires a manual summon.

### How to Spawn the Boss
To initiate the encounter, you must stand on the mini-boss spawner platform for several seconds. Once the summoning sequence completes, the Crimson Demon will materialize. Be warned: this boss is a significant step up from the standard mobs found on the island.

| Boss Statistic | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Boss Name** | Crimson Demon |
| **Level** | 50 |
| **Health Points (HP)** | 5,000 |
| **Special Mechanic** | Slime Splitting |
| **Primary Reward** | Chest Drop (contains Drake Egg) |

### Combat Strategy
The Crimson Demon is a level 50 entity, meaning players at the recommended island level of 30 will find this fight extremely punishing. The most difficult aspect of the fight is the splitting mechanic. Every time you deplete a portion of the boss's health, it splits into smaller, faster slimes. These smaller entities can quickly overwhelm a solo player if they are not managed with Area of Effect (AoE) attacks.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not attempt to tank the Crimson Demon if you are under level 40. Use "hit and run" tactics and keep your distance to avoid being swarmed by the smaller slimes.

## Obtaining and Hatching the Drake Egg

Once the Crimson Demon is defeated, it will drop a loot chest. This chest is your primary source for the Drake Egg. However, the drop is not guaranteed. You may need to defeat the boss multiple times to secure your pet.

### Drop Rates and RNG
The Drake Egg has a recorded drop rate of **9%**. In the world of Slime Seas, this is considered a "rare" drop, meaning you should expect to fight the boss approximately 10 to 12 times on average before seeing the egg.

| Item | Drop Chance | Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Drake Egg** | 9% | Rare |
| **Common Slime Loot** | 70% | Common |
| **Gold/Currency** | 100% | Guaranteed |

### The Hatching Process
After you have successfully obtained the Drake Egg from the chest, it will appear in your inventory. Unlike other games where pets hatch over time, Slime Seas requires active participation to hatch your **slime seas baby azure drake**.

1. **Equip the Egg**: Open your inventory and place the Drake Egg into an active slot.
2. **Engage in Combat**: You must deal damage to monsters while the egg is equipped.
3. **Accumulate Damage**: The egg absorbs the "energy" from your combat actions.
4. **Hatch**: Once the damage threshold is met, the egg will instantly hatch into the baby version of the drake.

> **💡 Tip:** To hatch the egg quickly, travel back to the starter island and pull large groups of low-level slimes. Use AoE abilities to maximize the damage dealt per second, which accelerates the hatching bar.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get the slime seas baby azure drake at level 1?**

A: While there is no hard level cap for entering the Crimson Ruins, the boss is level 50. You will likely be defeated instantly if you try to fight it at a very low level. It is recommended to be at least level 30-40 before attempting this.

**Q: Does the Drake Egg take up a pet slot while hatching?**

A: Yes, you must have the egg equipped in one of your active pet or utility slots for the damage you deal to count toward the hatching progress.

**Q: Can I trade the Drake Egg to other players?**

A: Currently, the Drake Egg is bound to the player who loots the chest. You must defeat the Crimson Demon yourself (or in a party) to obtain it.

**Q: How many times can the baby azure drake evolve?**

A: In the current 2026 version of Slime Seas, most pets have at least two evolution stages beyond their "baby" form, though this requires significant XP and specific evolution stones found in later islands.
