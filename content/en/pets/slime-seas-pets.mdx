## Locating the Crimson Ruins Island

The journey to obtaining your first high-tier pet begins with a voyage across the ocean. While many players spend their early hours on the starter island, the real progression starts once you hit level 30. The Crimson Ruins is the primary destination for players seeking their first major pet milestone.

The island itself is conveniently located right next to the starter island, making it easily accessible even for those with basic ships. However, do not let its proximity fool you; the difficulty spike is noticeable. You should ensure your gear is upgraded and you have reached at least the recommended level before attempting to farm for **slime seas pets** in this region.

## How to Summon the Crimson Demon

Once you have arrived at the Crimson Ruins, you must move inland. Ignore the standard mobs on the beach and head toward the center of the island. There, you will find a specialized mini-boss spawner. Unlike standard mobs that roam freely, the Crimson Demon requires a specific trigger to appear.

To summon the boss, you must stand directly on the mini-boss spawner for several seconds. This mechanic prevents accidental spawns and ensures that you are ready for the confrontation. Once the summoning is complete, the Crimson Demon will manifest, signaling the start of one of the most important fights for acquiring **slime seas pets**.

### Crimson Demon Boss Statistics

The Crimson Demon is a level 50 mini-boss, which can be quite a shock for players who just reached level 30. It possesses a high health pool and a unique mechanic that makes the fight progressively more chaotic.

| Stat | Value | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level** | 50 | High damage output for its tier |
| **Health** | 5,000 | Requires sustained DPS |
| **Ability** | **Slime Split** | Splits into smaller slimes upon death |
| **Drop Rate** | 9% | Chance to drop the **Drake Egg** |

When the Crimson Demon reaches zero health, it does not simply disappear. It splits into several smaller slimes. Each of these slimes must be defeated to successfully finish the encounter and claim your reward chest. This "split" mechanic can easily trap players who are low on health, so keep your distance and use area-of-effect (AoE) abilities if available.

## Obtaining and Hatching the Drake Egg

After successfully clearing the Crimson Demon and its smaller iterations, a chest will drop. This chest contains various loot items, but your primary goal is the Drake Egg. With a 9% drop rate, you may need to defeat the boss multiple times before the egg finally appears in your inventory. Persistence is key when farming for top-tier **slime seas pets**.

### The Hatching Process

Once you have obtained the Drake Egg, the work isn't over yet. The egg must be "hatched" through active gameplay. In Slime Seas, hatching isn't just about waiting on a timer; it requires participation.

1.  **Equip the Egg:** Navigate to your pet inventory and equip the Drake Egg as your active companion.
2.  **Engage in Combat:** You must deal damage to monsters while the egg is equipped.
3.  **Monitor Progress:** As you deal damage, the egg absorbs energy. The more damage you deal, the faster it hatches.
4.  **Hatch Completion:** Once the threshold is met, the egg will hatch into a **Baby Azure Drake**.

> **💡 Tip:** To hatch your egg as fast as possible, return to the starter island and pull large groups of low-level mobs. Since the requirement is based on damage instances and combat activity, clearing weak enemies in bulk is often faster than fighting single high-level targets.

## Strategies for Efficient Pet Farming

To build the ultimate collection of **slime seas pets**, you need a strategy that minimizes downtime. Farming a 9% drop can be tedious if you are not optimized.

### Group Farming
The Crimson Demon is significantly easier to handle with a group. Since the boss splits into multiple smaller targets, having one player focused on high single-target DPS for the main boss and another player using AoE skills for the splits can cut your farm time in half. Additionally, loot in Slime Seas is often instanced or distributed in a way that rewards cooperative play.

### Skill Synergy
Use weapons that have "Chain" or "Splash" properties. When the Crimson Demon splits, these weapons will hit all the smaller slimes simultaneously, preventing them from surrounding you and dealing chip damage.

### Inventory Management
Always clear your inventory before a farming session. You don't want to miss a Drake Egg drop because your "Misc" tab was full of low-value slime drops from the beach.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I have more than one pet equipped at a time?**

A: In the current 2026 version of Slime Seas, players start with one pet slot. However, you can unlock additional slots through specific game passes or by reaching high-tier milestones in the "Explorer" questline.

**Q: What happens if I die while the Drake Egg is equipped?**

A: Do not worry! You will not lose your Drake Egg if you die. It remains in your inventory. However, any progress toward hatching during that specific combat encounter might be lost, so try to stay alive to ensure your damage counts toward the hatch meter.

**Q: Is the 9% drop rate for the Drake Egg guaranteed?**

A: No, a 9% drop rate means that on average, you might see one egg every 11 or 12 boss kills. Because of RNG (random number generation), some players might get it on their first try, while others might need 20+ kills. Consistency is the key to getting the best **slime seas pets**.

**Q: Can I trade my Baby Azure Drake to other players?**

A: Trading mechanics for pets depend on the current server rules. Generally, hatched pets can be traded if they haven't been "Soulbound" through certain high-level upgrades, but eggs are often the most liquid currency in the player-to-player market.
